The throughline

Every system I built had a human on the other end

That shaped how I work. At sensen.ai my models produced court admissible enforcement tickets sent to real people. A model confusing a B for an 8 in bad lighting was not a wrong number on a dashboard, it was someone's REAL problem. We had cases where that went wrong and that's what I was building against. It taught me to stop trusting clean metrics and start asking the harder question. Does this still work 6 months after it's shipped?

My 2 research projects are 2 angles on that same instinct. The Multi-Agent Inference Reliability Framework asks whether a runtime monitor inside an agent loop actually helps and finds the exact point where it starts to hurt. Inference-Lens asks whether the automated judge we all rely on, can be fooled. The throughline is simple. I want AI systems that work the way they are supposed to, with measurable and trustworthy output at every layer.
