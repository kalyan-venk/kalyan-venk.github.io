The throughline

One question, two angles

My work circles a single idea. Every modern AI system leans on automated judges and monitors, and we treat them as if they are neutral and reliable. They often are not. Inference-Lens measures how easily an automated evaluator can be fooled. Agentic LLMOps measures when adding a monitor inside a live agent loop actually pays off and when it quietly backfires. Together they are one story about building AI systems you can trust, from the judge that grades outputs to the monitor that sits inside the pipeline.
