The clean scores are the finding

All 3 models land near 0.50 AUC-ROC on the held-out HH-RLHF test set. That is not a failure. It says surface features like length, readability and overlap do not reliably predict human preference.

Same benchmark, opposite robustness

Under the Neighbor attack, Logistic Regression's false-preference rate hits 78.4% (picks the worse answer 4 in 5). XGBoost, tied on the clean benchmark, barely moves. Architecture decides robustness, not the headline score.

Length ≠ quality

Unsupervised clustering found the most verbose-inconsistent archetype had the lowest chosen-rate (48.9%); the shorter conversational-engaging one the highest (51.6%).
